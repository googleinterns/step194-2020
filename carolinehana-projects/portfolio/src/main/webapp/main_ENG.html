My CS Background

Growing up during times of boredem I found my playing with a used and passed down engineering kit, making working cars and helicopters. This small hobby would encourage me to join the all girls robotics team in high school as part of the construction team. Robotics helped me grow to express myself and ideas more confidently in public, become a more creative problem solver, and make some awesome friends who were all incredibly talented. Of course using dangerous power tools was also a plus :). Going to college I knew I want to continue a carrer close to robotics and engineering. After doing Google's CSSI program last summer in NYC I was able to decide to pursue electrical engineering at Princeton. Although my experience with CS is limited I'm excited to explore what I can create.

IM WALKING HERE!-My experience growing up in the Big Apple

Jun 23, 2020

A lot of things in my life have shaped me. I am first generation student in my family who all have immigrated from Egypt. I loved growing up within my culture, I enjoy the traditions, the food, the people and the unique humor I was raised with. But I don’t think I would be proud of my family’s foreign status if it wasn't for New York City. I am lucky to have grown up in Queens, New York, known as the most diverse place in the world. Being around a plethora of cultures I never felt like the “other”, everyone freely expressed culture and were accepting of others. These cultures often condense into their own neighborhoods and have stores and restaurants solely dedicated to them. Most people are aware of larger ethnic neighborhoods like Little Italy or Chinatown, but there are dozens more! Koreatown, Little Guyana, Little India, Little Poland, and The Middle East of Queens are just a few colloquially known areas. The food in these areas are also unbelievable! Growing up in the city encouraged me to meet new people and curious to learn about their backgrounds, the stories are endless.

KNOCKOUT! MY EXPERIENCE IN THE WWE

Okay I wasn't actually in the WWE... but I did do wrestling!

Jun 23, 2020

“Hey don’t break your face on the mat today!”
As if my fear wasn’t through the roof already, my father’s statement quite easily cemented my enormous anxiety for my first wrestling match that day. But let me explain. For some students, freshman year is when you find your place, but for me It was when I dove head first into new experiences. Despite being incredibly unathletic I decided that sophomore year would be the first time I tried a new sport -- wrestling. Not a lot of girls were on the team and I was probably the most unfit. I fumbled on every move and in the first week alone I got bruises in twenty places and unimaginable soreness. The jokes my friends made about my upcoming “big WWE showcase” didn’t help much either. I was certain that I wanted to quit, but I continued attending each practice leading up to my first match.
One mat, one referee, two wrestlers and a whole crowd just watching,robotics competitions didn’t even feel as nearly intimidating as this. I ended up not performing my best that competition day but I continued the season, ending with a total of 4 match wins, a bunch of new friends and a whole new sense of confidence. Without wrestling literally and figuratively with my fears I never would’ve been the confident person I am now. Also with the career I intend to pursue, wrestling helped me become comfortable with taking risks and tackling problems bigger than I expect.
